FAQ & Troubleshooting

Quick answers to common questions and issues.

Common Questions

Does the app work without Telegram?
Yes. Without Telegram, the app still detects humans, sounds the alarm, and saves photos to your gallery. Telegram adds remote alerts and commands but is not required.
Does it work without internet?
The AI detection and alarm work completely offline. Photos are saved locally. Only Telegram features require internet (Wi-Fi or mobile data).
Can I use my daily phone as the security camera?
Yes, but the app needs the camera running continuously. Best to use a spare/old phone so your main phone stays available. Theft Mode works great on your daily phone though — arm it when you leave your phone unattended.
Does it detect animals?
The AI is trained specifically for humans. It won't detect cats, dogs, or other animals. This is by design — it reduces false alarms from pets.
How much storage do photos use?
Each photo is about 50-150KB (JPEG). At default settings (1 photo per second during detection), that's roughly 5-10MB per minute of active detection. Photos are organized in daily/hourly folders for easy cleanup.
Can multiple people control the cameras?
Yes. Set up a Telegram group, add your bot, and use the group's Chat ID. Everyone in the group can see alerts and send commands.
Can I use fingerprint instead of PIN to unlock?
Yes. Go to Settings → APP LOCK and enable "Fingerprint unlock". When the lock screen or theft countdown appears, the fingerprint prompt shows automatically. PIN is always available as a fallback. Note: face unlock is not supported because the app's camera is in use for detection. Fingerprint works on any device with a fingerprint sensor and an enrolled fingerprint.
Is my data sent to any server?
No. All AI processing happens on your phone. Photos are only sent to YOUR Telegram chat using YOUR bot. We have no servers and collect zero data. See our Privacy Policy.
What phones are supported?
Any Android phone running Android 8.0 or higher. The app uses GPU acceleration for AI detection on supported devices, with automatic CPU fallback on others. Tested on Samsung Galaxy A50 (Exynos 9610, Android 11) and Galaxy S22 (Snapdragon 8 Gen 1, Android 14).
How does GPU acceleration work?
The app automatically detects your phone's GPU capabilities at startup. If GPU acceleration is available, it uses it — no configuration needed. GPU mode can be up to 10x faster than CPU on supported devices. You can verify which mode is active by sending /status via Telegram — it shows "Engine: YOLOv8n/GPU" or "Engine: YOLOv8n/CPU". All Snapdragon, Exynos, and MediaTek chipsets with OpenCL GPU support are compatible.

Troubleshooting

Detection stops after a few hours
Samsung and other manufacturers kill background apps aggressively. Fix: Settings → Apps → HumanDetector AI → Battery → Unrestricted. Also keep the phone plugged in. Enable Heartbeat (every 15 min) so you know if the app is still running.
Too many false positives
Try these in order: 1) Enable Face Verification (filters 50-75% of false positives). 2) Raise confidence to 55%. 3) Increase consecutive frames to 4 or 5. 4) Avoid pointing at TVs or laptops — moving images trigger detection.
Telegram test connection fails
Check that: 1) Bot token has no extra spaces. 2) You sent /start to the bot first. 3) Chat ID is correct (use getUpdates URL). 4) Phone has internet access. 5) Tap the 👁 eye button to reveal and verify the token.
Commands not working via Telegram
Commands are polled every 7 seconds — wait a moment. Check: 1) Bot token and chat ID are correct. 2) Device Name matches exactly (case-insensitive). 3) Send /status to verify the phone is online.
Commands not working — using same phone for app and Telegram
Telegram commands are for remote control from a separate device. If the app and Telegram are on the same phone, switching to Telegram puts the app in the background and commands won't be processed. Use a computer browser (web.telegram.org), a tablet, or a second phone to send commands. If you only have one phone, control the app directly through its on-screen buttons and Settings.
Face Verification misses real humans
FV requires a visible face. If the person is facing away, wearing a full face covering, or the camera angle only shows the top of their head, FV will filter them out. Send /fv_off_NAME to disable it in those situations.
Grab detection too sensitive / not sensitive enough
Grab detection uses a fixed sensitivity tuned for stationary placement. The phone must be still for about 5 seconds (calibration phase) before detection activates. Small movements while holding the phone do not trigger it — only a clear pickup does. If placed on a vibrating surface (e.g., near a washing machine), try repositioning to a more stable spot.
Black screen on first launch
The consent dialog may delay camera startup. Tap Accept, then wait a moment. If the camera still doesn't start, tap the Flip button twice (flip to front camera, then back). This forces a camera restart.
App crashes on start
Try: 1) Clear app data (Settings → Apps → HumanDetector AI → Storage → Clear Data). 2) Reinstall the app. 3) If the problem persists, email us with your phone model and Android version.
